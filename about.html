SKIT Autonomous SGPA Guide — Grade Points and Credit System Explained

SKIT (Swami Keshvanand Institute of Technology) Jaipur operates under an autonomous examination system, separate from the standard RTU result format. SKIT results include Internal (ISE) and External (SEE) marks for each subject, and the grading uses a 10-point scale where the overall grade for a subject is derived from the total of both components.

In the SKIT autonomous grading scheme, a total of 91–100 marks earns grade O (Outstanding) with 10 grade points. A total of 81–90 earns A+ with 9 grade points, 71–80 earns A with 8 grade points, 61–70 earns B+ with 7 grade points, 51–60 earns B with 6 grade points, 41–50 earns C with 5 grade points, and 33–40 earns D with 4 grade points. A FAIL remark overrides the marks and assigns 0 grade points regardless of total score.

SGPA is calculated as the weighted average: Sum of (Grade Points × Credits) divided by Total Credits. Credits vary by subject type — core theory subjects carry 3–4 credits, lab subjects carry 1–2 credits, professional development and industrial training components carry 1 credit, and SODECA (Social Outreach, Discipline and Extra-Curricular Activities) carries 0.5 credits. Subjects with 0 credits such as audit courses and technical training are excluded from the SGPA calculation.

A key difference from RTU results: SKIT does not provide a single overall REMARKS line at the bottom of the result. BunkWise derives your overall PASS/FAIL status by checking every subject's individual remarks field — if any subject carries a FAIL, your overall result is marked as FAIL. This matches the actual academic consequence at SKIT, where failing even one subject requires that subject to be cleared before promotion.

The SKIT result PDF is processed using BunkWise's AI-powered extraction pipeline, which reads your ISE and SEE marks, corrects common OCR spelling errors in subject names, and matches each subject to the credit map for your branch and year. The AI pipeline is powered by Google Gemini and runs through a secure Cloudflare Worker proxy — your result data is sent for AI extraction but is not stored by BunkWise.

Download your result report to keep a formatted copy of your SGPA analysis. The downloaded PDF includes your name, roll number, subject-wise breakdown, grade points, credit points, and the final SGPA — formatted for easy reading and sharing.

Frequently Asked Questions — SKIT Autonomous SGPA

How does SKIT autonomous grading differ from RTU grading?

RTU results use letter grades (A++, A+, A, B+…) directly on the mark-sheet. SKIT autonomous results show ISE (Internal) and SEE (External) marks, and BunkWise derives the grade by summing both components and applying the SKIT grade boundary table. The grade point scale is also slightly different — SKIT uses O for Outstanding (10 GP) which does not exist in the standard RTU scheme.

Why does my SGPA differ from what I calculated manually?

The most common reason is a credit mismatch — a subject in your result was not matched to the credit database for your branch, so it was excluded from the SGPA calculation. Check the Credits column in your result table for any dashes. Other reasons include rounding differences in the total marks or a grade boundary edge case where your total sits exactly at a boundary and BunkWise and your institution apply different rounding rules.

Is my result data safe when using the AI pipeline?

The text extracted from your PDF is sent to Google's Gemini AI API for analysis via a secure Cloudflare Worker proxy. The API key is never exposed in the browser. BunkWise does not store your result data — it is only processed in transit. After the result is computed, it is stored in your browser's localStorage for up to 24 hours so you can return to this result page. After 24 hours the data expires automatically.

What is the SODECA subject and how does it affect SGPA?

SODECA stands for Social Outreach, Discipline and Extra-Curricular Activities. It is a 0.5-credit subject at SKIT. Because it carries credits, your grade in SODECA does contribute a small amount to your SGPA calculation. A FAIL in SODECA will also mark your overall result as FAIL since BunkWise checks all subjects for the PASS/FAIL determination.

What do Soft Skill Training and Technical Training subjects count for?

Soft Skill Training and Technical Training are listed in SKIT results but carry 0 credits. BunkWise includes them in the result table for completeness but excludes them from the SGPA calculation since they do not contribute credit weight. Their grade points, if any, do not affect your final SGPA.
